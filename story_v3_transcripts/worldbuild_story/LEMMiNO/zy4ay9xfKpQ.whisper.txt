Just let me know. Oceans cover a significant portion of the earth and as the spin off a fish, it's no surprise that mankind really loves the ocean. It allows us to cool off in the summer, produce life-giving oxygen, and much like whales can often be found below sea level. Because of this, we have learned to treat the ocean with a care and respect, such a delicate life-essential system deserves. It's what I would have said had this been a parallel universe in which things made sense. Unfortunately, we live in the other universe. Because the ocean is going through a bit of a rough patch at the moment, fellow creators Mr. Beast and Mark Rober have launched a campaign known as Team Seas. It's a kind of sequel to the Team Trees initiative which successfully raised more than 20 million dollars to plant over 20 million trees back in 2019. But this time around, the goal is to raise 30 million dollars to remove 30 million pounds of trash and plastic from the ocean, beaches and rivers. Which is why almost everyone you follow seems to develop a very sudden passion for oceanography. In the grand scheme of things, removing 30 million pounds of trash and plastic is not going to change the world. Billions of pounds of trash is dumped into the ocean every single year. It will, however, bring awareness to an issue which affects every single one of us. And perhaps with some luck that awareness will inspire those with a power to effect lasting change. If not today, then perhaps tomorrow. Who knows? I think it's a worthy cause at least, so I will be donating $1,000. If you'd like to donate yourself, you should head over to Team Seas.org.
